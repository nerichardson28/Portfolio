Guided Reading




Theory

Guided Reading is a good way to find out a student's reading level, expose them to a higher reading level and help them get to that level by exposing them to different kinds of books and working with them to read those books .

Benefits/Positives

-Personalized instruction
- Confidence Building
- Addresses specific reading needs
-Provides access to different and challenging books

Drawbacks/Challeneges

-Students might try to stay in the lower level groups because it's easier

Grade/Unit/Outcome

Grade: K-6
Subject:ELA
Outcome:To help the student reach a higher level of reading

Summary

Guided Reading is a great way of helping students reach a higher learning level without singling them out, exposes them to other books and gives them the confidence to try and reach a higher level of reading
