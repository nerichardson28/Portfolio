Instructional Methods Portfolio

Jigsaw




Theory

Students become "experts" on a small piece of information and then teach it to their group.

Benefits/Positives

- Students are responsible for learning
- Students socialize with classmates
- Student actively engaged
- teacher can move around the room
- teacher can answer questions

Drawbacks/Challeneges

- Planning is time consuming
- Uses up time in class
- Groups can be uneven

Grade/Subject/Outcome

Grade: I would use this in grades 8-12
Subject:I think it would work well
in psychology/ELA/Social Studies
when discussing different topics
Outcome:Will be used to cover more
topics in a short amount of time

Summary

Jigsaw is a student lead activity where students become experts on a topic and teach it to their classmates. It can be time consuming initially, but worth it once you see the learning that comes of it.
